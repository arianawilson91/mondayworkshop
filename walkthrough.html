PART 2 · COWORK

What is Cowork?

Chat answers questions. Cowork does tasks — it works directly with real files in a folder you choose: spreadsheets, Word docs, slides, PDFs. It's a tab inside the Claude Desktop app (Mac & Windows) and it's included in the Pro plan everyone just set up.

  • Give it a folder + a plain-English task
  • It shows you a plan first, then works step by step
  • It creates real files — .xlsx, .docx, .pptx, .pdf
  • Long tasks keep running even if you close your laptop

Presenter notes

Timing

Full auto-play runs about two minutes — good as a looping intro while people arrive. Presenting live, budget 10–12 minutes for Part 1 (everyone signs up on their own device) and 15–20 minutes for Part 2 (install check, folder setup, and one real Cowork task).

The Cowork demo

Cowork is included with Pro — no extra purchase. It lives in the Claude Desktop app (claude.com/download), not the browser. Bring a “Brand Content” folder — a dozen sample product photos plus a small products.xlsx — share it before the session, and have everyone run the same content-calendar task. It's contained, low-stakes, and produces a real Excel file they keep. Tip: attendees with a real business can point Cowork at their own photos instead and leave with a usable calendar.

The payment step

This is the one step you can't do for attendees and shouldn't do on a shared screen. Show the mock slide on the projector, then have each person complete real checkout privately on their own laptop or phone.

Turning this into a video file

Play this page full-screen and screen-record it: on a Mac, press Cmd+Shift+5 → "Record Selected Portion" → press Play here → stop when it loops. That gives you an .mp4/.mov you can drop into slides or share before the meeting.

Common snags

Email code lands in spam; school/work Google accounts blocked by an admin (fall back to email signup); card declines on university purchasing cards. Mention all three up front and the Q&A gets much shorter.
